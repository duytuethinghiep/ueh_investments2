## 📖 4. Course Description

This course extends fundamental investment knowledge into advanced and applied domains.

Key focus areas:

* Advanced asset classes such as derivatives and alternative investments
* Investment funds, private equity, real estate, commodities
* International diversification strategies
* Modern portfolio construction and advanced investment models
* Technical analysis and quantitative investment tools
* Use of technology in investment decision-making
* Basic legal framework in investment and procurement activities

The course emphasizes practical application, analytical thinking, and independent learning.

---

## 🎯 5. Learning Outcomes (CLOs)

### 5.1 Knowledge (CLO1)

Students will be able to:

* Compare alternative investments and traditional investments
* Explain mutual funds, private equity, real estate, commodities, and other alternative assets
* Identify valuation issues in domestic and international investment environments
* Calculate risk and return for alternative investment assets
* Apply advanced asset pricing models
* Explain derivatives: types, characteristics, and market mechanisms
* Summarize technical analysis principles
* Apply risk management theories in portfolio planning
* Identify key technologies used in investment analysis
* Understand legal foundations of investment and procurement

### 5.2 Skills (CLO2)

Students will be able to:

* Write analytical reports on alternative investments
* Value assets and make investment decisions
* Develop strategies for derivatives markets
* Combine technical analysis with quantitative models
* Use Excel, R, Python, and related tools in analysis
* Participate in group discussions and presentations
* Read international financial literature

### 5.3 Autonomy & Responsibility (CLO3)

Students will be able to:

* Work independently and in teams
* Lead or support group activities
* Defend analytical and investment decisions
* Continuously update knowledge through modern resources
